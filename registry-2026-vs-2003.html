Does current policy actually prevent harm?

A forthcoming analysis asking whether the policies built to address sexual harm are designed to reduce it, and what evidence exists either way.

This page is a placeholder. An earlier version of this article was written around a different editorial frame and has been withdrawn pending rewrite. The URL is preserved so existing links continue to resolve.

What the replacement will cover

  • What outcome each major policy was enacted to produce, and whether that outcome is measured
  • Whether policy design distinguishes individuals by assessed risk or applies uniformly by category
  • What evidence was before legislatures at enactment, and whether it has been revisited
  • How prevention effectiveness should be measured, and which measures are currently collected
  • Where the evidence supports current policy, where it does not, and where it is simply absent

Evidence standard

The replacement will carry confidence ratings under the Research Charter, and every claim will be traceable to a primary source before publication.
