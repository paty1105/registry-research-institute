Library

Research Library Framework

This section will become PRI's searchable evidence database on what prevents sexual harm and what the evidence says about the policies meant to address it.

Each record will include title, author, year, source type, summary, key findings, stated limitations, full citation, and related topics. Records will carry the confidence ratings set out in the Research Charter, so a reader can see at a glance whether a claim rests on multiple systematic reviews or on a single early study.

Planned Collections

Ordered by prevention level, from before an offense occurs to after one has.

Primary Prevention

  • Evaluations of school and community prevention education programs
  • Organizational safeguarding standards and situational prevention research
  • Bystander and adult-responsibility intervention studies
  • Confidential help-seeking services and their reported outcomes
  • Public awareness campaigns and measured effects

Risk, Protective Factors, and Treatment

  • Protective factor research: housing, employment, family stability, social connection
  • Treatment outcome studies and meta-analyses
  • Risk assessment instruments and validation studies
  • Peer-reviewed desistance and aging research
  • Treatment access, capacity, and waiting time data

Policy, Law, and Outcomes

  • Federal and state court opinions
  • Government reports, audits, and appropriations analyses
  • Supervision, registration, and notification policy research
  • Recidivism studies, with categories clearly separated
  • Law review articles and expert testimony
  • International comparison materials

The First Records

The opening set is deliberately balanced across the three collections, so the library reflects the Institute's scope from the first day rather than accumulating toward it.

Prevention

Systematic reviews of school-based prevention programs, situational and organizational prevention research, the Charité Dunkelfeld program literature, and evaluations of confidential help-seeking services.

Risk and Treatment

Hanson desistance research, Static-99R materials and validation studies, treatment outcome meta-analyses, and protective factor research on housing, employment, and family stability.

Policy and Law

Smith v. Doe, Gundy v. United States, the OPPAGA program review, WSIPP cost-benefit analyses, residency restriction studies, and DOJ SMART Office compliance records.

Submissions welcome. If you have research, a court record, a state audit, or a dataset that belongs here, send it through the contact page. Corrections to existing records are treated as a priority.
