Prevention Science

Prevention science asks what actually changes behavior: which factors raise risk, which factors protect against it, which interventions work, and how risk moves over the course of a life. These are empirical questions with a substantial research literature behind them.

This section organizes that literature. Where the evidence is strong, PRI says so. Where a widely repeated claim is not well supported, PRI says that too, and applies the confidence ratings set out in the Research Charter.
