Visual Evidence Lab

Turning prevention evidence into something you can cite.

Citation-ready graphics for attorneys, journalists, legislators, researchers, educators, and families.

Prevention arguments fail when the underlying numbers are unclear. These modules do the clarifying work: showing where prevention resources actually go, where harm actually occurs, how risk changes over a life, and what a safety claim has to demonstrate before it can be believed. Each module is built to be cited, and none publishes until its figures are sourced.

Graphic 01

Where Prevention Money Goes

Public spending on sexual harm is concentrated at the last stage, after an offense has already occurred. This module maps the distribution across all three prevention levels using published appropriations and audit data.

Primary

Education, safeguarding, adult responsibility, and confidential help before harm. Acts on the whole population; measured by the incidence of first offenses.

Secondary

Early identification, treatment access, and situational intervention where risk is elevated but no offense has occurred.

Tertiary

Treatment, supervision, monitoring, and reintegration after an offense. Where nearly all current expenditure sits.

Research note: will not publish until appropriations figures for each level are sourced to state or federal budget documents. See the Prevention Framework for the model and Policy Impact for the cost evidence gathered so far.

Where First Offenses Actually Occur

Prevention effort is often aimed at the stranger. This module will map where harm is documented to occur, by relationship and setting, so prevention resources can be aimed at the same place.

By relationship

Documented distribution of who causes harm relative to the child, drawn from primary victimization data.

By setting

Where harm occurs: home, youth-serving organizations, online, and other institutional settings.

Prior contact

What proportion of those who cause harm were known to any system beforehand, and what that implies for monitoring-based strategies.

Graphic 03 — In Development

What a Prevention Claim Requires

Prevention arguments collapse when the underlying statistics mix categories. This module will set out what must be reported separately for any safety claim to be testable.

New victimization

Allegations involving a new victim. The only category that measures whether harm was prevented.

Administrative events

Reporting and compliance failures with no new victim. Frequently folded into headline figures.

The reporting standard

A proposed disclosure format for jurisdictions that want their prevention claims to be verifiable.

What is measured
