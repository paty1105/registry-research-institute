What states actually fund to prevent harm.

A forthcoming comparison of what states spend on preventing a first offense against what they spend on responding after one, drawn from published appropriations and audit records.

This page is a placeholder. An earlier version of this article was written around a different editorial frame and has been withdrawn pending rewrite. The URL is preserved so existing links continue to resolve.

What the replacement will cover

  • State appropriations for primary prevention, prevention education, and victim services
  • Funded treatment capacity, access, and waiting times
  • Federal funding streams available for prevention and how much states draw down
  • What states report as outcomes, and how many report any outcome at all
  • Which states have shifted investment toward prevention, and what they have published about results

Evidence standard

The replacement will carry confidence ratings under the Research Charter, and every claim will be traceable to a primary source before publication.
