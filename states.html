State Policy Profiles

Planned profiles will document, for every state, both sides of the ledger: what the state requires of people under supervision, and what the state funds in prevention and treatment. Built from primary legal sources and published budget documents.

What Each Profile Will Track

Prevention and Treatment Investment

What the state funds to stop harm before it happens, and what it funds to treat those who have caused it.

  • Appropriations for primary prevention, prevention education, and victim services
  • Whether school-based prevention education is mandated, funded, and evaluated
  • Funded treatment capacity, access criteria, and waiting times
  • Safeguarding requirements for youth-serving organizations
  • Availability of confidential help for adults seeking it before causing harm
  • Federal prevention funding available to the state, and how much is drawn down

Outcome Measurement

Whether the state can demonstrate that any of its approach works.

  • Whether outcome data is collected at all, and at what interval
  • Whether new victimization is reported separately from administrative events
  • Whether any published evaluation of state programs exists

Obligations and Restrictions

What the state requires of people under supervision, and what those requirements cost to administer.

  • Duration of supervision obligations and available review pathways
  • In-person reporting frequency
  • Residence and exclusion-zone restrictions
  • Internet identifier reporting
  • Travel and temporary lodging reporting
  • Driver’s license or identification markings
  • Public website disclosures
  • Penalties for noncompliance
