About

Evidence That Prevents Harm.

The Prevention Research Institute is an independent educational research initiative examining what actually prevents sexual harm, and whether the policies built to address it produce the safety they promise.

PRI is built around a simple premise: fewer victims is the only outcome that counts, and a policy should be able to show that it produces it. That means attending to demonstrated risk factors, protective factors, treatment access, and measured results rather than to assumptions about what ought to work.

Where We Concentrate

Prevention research divides interventions into three levels by when they act. Every system that responds after harm has occurred is, by definition, too late for that victim.

  • Primary: before any offense, aimed at the whole population. Education, adult responsibility, organizational safeguarding, and confidential help before harm occurs.
  • Secondary: after risk appears but before harm, aimed at identified individuals and settings.
  • Tertiary: after an offense, aimed at preventing another. Treatment, supervision, reintegration, and monitoring.

Nearly all current public expenditure in this area sits at the third level. PRI documents that allocation and asks what the first level could do with a share of it. The framework is set out on the Prevention Framework page, and its practical form for communities and organizations on Preventing a First Offense.

Editorial Standard: PRI separates research summaries, legal analysis, commentary, and personal narrative. Every factual claim should be traceable to a source.

Mission

To advance evidence-based prevention through research, public education, policy analysis, and practical strategies that reduce harm, support accountability, and strengthen long-term community safety.

What PRI Is Not

PRI does not provide legal advice or representation, and cannot refer anyone to counsel. It is not a crisis service. It does not campaign for or against specific legislation. Where evidence is contested, PRI reports the disagreement rather than resolving it in favor of a preferred conclusion, and where a widely repeated claim is not well supported, it says so regardless of which argument the claim serves.

Core Values

  • Accuracy over rhetoric
  • Transparency in sourcing
  • Fair representation of competing evidence
  • Prevention measured by real-world outcomes
  • Human dignity for victims, families, people in treatment, and affected communities
