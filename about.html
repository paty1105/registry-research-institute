About

Evidence That Prevents Harm.

The Prevention Research Institute is an independent educational research initiative dedicated to collecting, organizing, and communicating evidence about preventing sexual harm and reducing reoffending.

PRI is built around a simple premise: public safety improves when policy addresses demonstrated risk factors, strengthens protective factors, supports effective treatment and accountability, and measures real-world outcomes.

Editorial Standard: PRI separates research summaries, legal analysis, commentary, and personal narrative. Every factual claim should be traceable to a source.

Mission

To advance evidence-based prevention through research, public education, policy analysis, and practical strategies that reduce harm, support accountability, and strengthen long-term community safety.

Core Values

  • Accuracy over rhetoric
  • Transparency in sourcing
  • Fair representation of competing evidence
  • Prevention measured by real-world outcomes
  • Human dignity for victims, families, people in treatment, and affected communities
